Cataract Surgery Clinic

  • January 10, 2022
  • 69 people
  • Latest news
  • Call Connect

Once a cataract has been diagnosed, the patient's treatment options are primarily surgical. Surgery can be performed when a patient's vision has worsened to the extent that it is interfering with his daily activities (no longer is it required for the cataract to become mature). However, in some cases, where there are linked issues or potential complications, an early (even urgent) operation may be required; in these cases, you should follow your Consulting Eye Surgeon's advice.
